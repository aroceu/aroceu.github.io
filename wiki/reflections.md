# reflections

## principles (condensed)

- To be true to myself without any shame for my past mistakes, imperfections, perspectives, and who I am. I am always growing, but there's no use having shame for the person I used to be in order to be the person I am now. I can really only control myself, so it's important for me to understand and embrace who I am and live with authenticity.
- To be open-minded to the perspectives and humanity of others based on their individualities as unique human beings.
- To be patient and think long-term in order to hold dialogue and build community and be part of a process, regardless of the situation, because the only way any of us can grow is if we're willing to find common ground and grow together.
- To avoid violence, reactivity, and bad faith, as those are ways to get more people hurt, not heal.
- To make sure that not only truths but also facts are honored, and not make a judgment based on knowledge that has not been thoroughly researched and contextualized.
- To try new things, always.
- To live with style and fun and enjoy myself for who I am, and encourage others to do the same.

## principles (thoughtdump - my politics and how they align with my personal values)

- I believe in second chances, that the only certain thing is time but usually not people
- I believe in letting people make mistakes and giving them the ability to have the willingness to grow, the same way I give myself that same grace
- I believe in rehabilitation, that in many cases people don't necessarily need forgiveness extended to them to improve, but at least time and kindness from those who are willing to offer it
- I would rather make the mistake of being too kind than make the mistake of being too cruel
- I believe that we are all unique human beings with individual experiences that color the way we see and interact with the world; and though I may not fully understand someone else's perspective, im willing to understand them as a human being, especially as then they are more willing to understand mine
- I believe in putting the same energy in the world as I want out of it
- I believe that "letting go" means making peace - not forgiving, not forgetting, but accepting that something happened and not letting it define how you live in the future
- I believe in prison abolishment and restorative - but not punitive - justice. I believe that both can coexist
- I believe in the separation of my personal experiences/feelings/preferences and my own beliefs in these truths
- I believe that we have far more in common than we think; that letting our differences define our relationships oftentimes leads to Othering; that we gain more and learn more about ourselves (and others) when we try to see eye to eye with someone else, no matter how different we feel to them
- I believe that Othering is inherently dehumanizing, because it positions a person or a group of people further in a way from you wherein you can regard them as someone fundamentally different from you, even though despite their feelings or experiences they are still as human as you; which leads to things like superiority/moral complexes, putting others on a pedestal/worship. I believe in not seeing myself as better than other people, but the same, because I have as much ability to do/say the things they do for me, on a fundamental level
- I believe in the importance of personal choice, partially because I'm an American but also because I believe in everyone being able to live out their truths - emotional and otherwise - as much as me. I don't believe in strong arming people or bullying individuals or guilting other humans into believing the same things as me or making the same choices that I do. (I was also raised Christian, so I experienced enough of that as it is.) Instead, I believe in keeping myself informed and trying to inform those who are willing to listen, but allowing people to think for themselves and make decisions that they want to make, not decisions that I want them to make. I dont believe in making decisions for other people or trying to control them or their lifestyles 
- I also do not believe in any human categorization or bioessentialist ideals because of this - I use personality systems to help myself write and for my anxieties to understand that people don't think in the same way as me (and horoscopes for fun) - but fundamentally I believe we all have the ability to choose and change, and should not let our understanding of others be based in something that they cannot change
- I believe that I am a flawed and inconsistent and hypocritical human being because we all are; but just as much we are capable of changing and growing as well, not necessarily out of these fundamental human traits but instead based on our character and who we want to be
- I believe that learning is a life long process that we can only do together, and that shunning people for not knowing enough or being unwilling to help those who are willing to learn is fundamentally anti-progressive
- I believe in being well-informed and fact checking, not just to be knowledgeable, but to better develop my opinions and viewpoints based on my understanding of the world. I also believe that any content consumption is not unquelling support, but rather keeping yourself in the know 
- I believe that a lot of times things just *are*, and don't have to exist with judgment or intent
- but I also believe that intent is why we're here, why we do the things we do, why any of this matters
- I believe in nuance - that for every black and white there is always a grey, and that, broadly speaking, grey areas are wider and thus contain more universal truths than either end of any spectrum
- I believe in unassociating from people who were fundamentally unkind to me, not for any political reason but because I understand my emotional truth and know that no matter what I may believe on a humanist level, I am not obligated to extend a hand to those who, as it felt to me, were unapologetically dehumanizing toward me
- I am a humanist and pacifist; and a stoicist when it comes to my personal lifestyle
- I believe that the first solution is reason, and violence, in a sense, can be resorted to when people can't be reasoned with. However, I also know that I do not have the capacity to execute that violence, nor do I ever want to
- I believe in the eradication of systems that seek to disenfranchise, exploit, and ultimately harm people who have no way of fighting back. 
- I believe that America is a fundmentally colonialist system particularly due to its military industrial complex. I also believe that it has enough money to make many people believe they don't live under an authoritarian government when ultimately the American system (due to the power that corporations and money wields) has more in common with authoritarianism than the democratic republic it believes itself to be
- I believe that fear will drive us apart but empathy is the only way we can come together. I believe that anger is not a negative emotion, but a neutral one &mdash; it can be executed out of fear, but it can also motivate empathy.
