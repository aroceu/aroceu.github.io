# my youtube rpf ot3: a history

SmallAnt and Fir both started as Super Mario Odyssey speedrunners circa 2017~2018. SmallAnt's specialty for a while was mincaps - minimum captures, a specific challenge that required you to use as minimal captures (which is Super Mario Odyssey's main mechanic) to beat the game in the fastest time possible. Fir's specialty, meanwhile, was All Moons, a challenge that required you to get all 880 of the moons in Super Mario Odyssey. They were aware of each other's existence at this time - they followed each other on Twitter - but publicly rarely interacted outside of that, likely just to normal acquaintenceship.

CJ got into Youtube by watching SmallAnt, actually. SmallAnt's SMO speedruns, to be specific—as he puts it, he saw SmallAnt play and thought to himself "I could do that" and then that's what he did. He did, in short, start as a SmallAnt fan before becoming his friend. He got into the scene after doing a lot of successful speedruns, and ended up doing a lot of collabs with SmallAnt and some of his friends on the side. <small>(He also made a cover of Lucid Dreams by Juice World called "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mu3fCba0_FY">Nut Jump Dreams</a>" and Roses by Juice World called "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=05JwWaAEFAg">Nut Jump Goodbyes</a>." This will be relevant later.)</small> Though it's not the first collab CJ did, with anyone or with SmallAnt, the video that put him on the map was <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSDbhI24qHI">"I got sent a broken copy of Mario Odyssey"</a>, a randomizer SMO challenge race he did against SmallAnt. SmallAnt posted his own version of the video a month later (likely to allow CJ to accrue views organically), <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=58AmCK-EZ50">I challenged the Mario Speedrun Master...and won. Here's how</a>, and directed viewers to CJ's channel afterward.

Fir did not do collabs with them in this time, though I believe he has videos from this period referencing their or SmallAnt's existence. In all fairness, Fir was not really a content creator at this point, just a speedrunner. FIr's own content creation career actually started about the time they started doing content together.

And this is where we come to Super Mario Hide & Seek—perhaps one of my favorite things to exist on Youtube, ever. A modder who SmallAnt knew, CraftyBoss, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UocYHtuzWZA">created a mod</a> that allowed Super Mario Odyssey to be played multiplayer online, which SMO was not originally intended to do. (He did this totally and fully legally btw.) In this playtest, SmallAnt suggested that he and Crafty play Hide and Seek—which would come as a fully fledged idea barely a month later.

On November 19, 2021, a legend was born: SmallAnt posted "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CATm2QWj9U8">Mario Odyssey's Online Multiplayer is Amazing</a>", wherein he began playing Super Mario Odyssey Hide & Seek with not only CraftyBoss, but also CJ and Fir. You see where this is going. The four of them were the first to do it—<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SStdceQJKzU">CJ</a> and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OoGtiUrizuY">Fir</a> posted their own on the same day as SmallAnt's, so viewers could watch all three different hide and seek sessions across their channels. (As someone who already watched all three of them, this made me very happy.)

They continued to play a lot of SMO Hide and Seek through the end of 2021, and into 2022. There were also many additional modifications to the game—one where they <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ysVoQzxm748">didn't have Cappy</a> (which then didn't allow them to do any captures), another when they <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uAJ3QTapCUE&t=517s">were 2D</a>,   <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G_ziY9jKYJI>smaller</a>, played <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EN_CwNMbyPo">freeze tag</a>, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RcWjZcy5hBs&t=26s">prop hunt</a>, sardines, ones that involved messing with gravity, wall running, and even time travel, and so on and so forth. It is an incredibly fun series which I will always recommend on Youtube, even if you've never played SMO. I'm pretty sure I'm insanely biased because I've been watching all these guys from the beginning and I absolutely adore SMO just as a base game, but whatever. It's good content!

Regardless, the TL;DR is this:

- SmallAnt and Fir have known each other the longest, since at least 2018, both as speedrunners in the same game even in different categories
- CJ got into streaming/content creation at the end of 2019/roughly the beginning of 2020 after watching SmallAnt play
- CJ and SmallAnt started collabing together a lot due to both of their different yet proficient skillsets at speedrunning the game, including races, challenges, and manhunt
- Fir remained in SmallAnt's orbit (and even CJ's after a bit, shortly before Hide and Seek - <a href="https://x.com/SimplyFir/status/1431782061375885316">CJ won a competition that Fir hosted</a> on August 28, 2021, a couple of months before the first Hide and Seek video) but put more energy towards speedrunning and streams rather than content creation
- The three of them put SMO Hide and Seek on the map in November 2021
- CJ and Smant continue to collab, while Fir establishes his own content creation style by himself through a lot of 2022
- In 2023, Fir starts collabing with them in his own videos, soft-launching the idea of a custom-made escape room
    - It bears saying that Fir's SMO escape rooms inspired SmallAnt to make his legendary <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u4LfOHLpPbc">Pokemon Escape Rooms</a> (source: trust me. but the timeline works out)
- This quickly escalates to them trying a Pokemon Soul Link together in the middle of 2023, a 3 player version of a Nuzlocke; CJ and SmallAnt had done a few previous over the past couple of years
- The three of them continue doing collaborations and challenges together through the rest of 2023 and all throughout 2024, in a variety of SMO, Pokemon, and game shows - either between the three of them, or some permutation of a pair of them. They also at this very moment have more collaborations in the works, as they are streaming at least once a week with each other.
