- essentially killing enemies is just sending them back to spawn and forcing them to lose their special gauge. if they have to back up and fail to use their special to push you away, it is almost as good as having killed them. (if they back up, don’t push too hard; keep an eye out for their special! or their teammates, who they are usually backing up to for help!)
- don’t get distracted by someone you spot as a free kill if they are not in a position that you want to be in or that needs to be protected. take note of them but don’t chase after them— you may be leaving objective unattended and slow your push down
- use bombs if someone is out of range rather than facing them head-on with your main weapon. use specials instead if this is during a push.

- if there is a backliner on the other team, play close enough to your backliner that the other backliner has to worry about your backliner instead of you. (if the other backliner does not see you, then you can poke them with bombs or over ledges; otherwise respect them and don’t stray from your own backliner)

- let enemy use specials to prolong the push but don’t die or panic toward objective; it is ok if you lose the lead as long as you stay alive. afterward, see if you can punish enemy for the positions they’ve taken (together) or use subsequent specials to push them out or stall out the objective. coordinate specials with fights rather than everyone using specials at the same time

## rainmaker
only hold onto rainmaker:
a) if making push (mid control; specials; advantage)
b) can get check
c) getting more points to lead
d) in attacking position (mid control) and stalling
e) if winning, last second (if able) to prevent opposing team to make an overtime push

otherwise:
- pop but don't pick up (for paint, rainmaker is in our base/area, not winning enough fights to guarantee a takeback over mid, there is still time left in the game and we're losing) / let rainmaker reset (gives team better chances to either win fights or lure the other team to grabbing it and then splatting them)
- reset rainmaker (only if rm is really close to getting lead or too close to a ko AND team is not losing fights)
- don't pop or pick up (we're winning, there's not a lot of time left in the game, rainmaker is an area that we control)

Tc
1. Know the tower route/map
2. Know your role and everyone else's (riding tower, pushing forward, guarding tower)
3. Know the tower "phases" and what phase is currently occurring

Phases:
Fighting over mid (in a good or equally matched game, this should occur the most)
Push/fighting over defending team's territory
- 2 around/behind tower, and 2 up front
- The 1 around tower is there to make the checkpoint breaks faster, but should otherwise guard the tower and tower rider (sometimes can also push up front depending on how much control the team has on the map)
Push/fighting over attacking team's territory
- if any of the other team have players pushed up front, 1-2 people (sometimes 3 if needed, depending) should be flushing them out, NOT going for tower
- The rest should be trying to stop the tower push or at least pressure the other team off by using specials and throwing bombs at tower—don't outright attack with a short range weapon UNLESS they cannot see you, have no idea, or are too busy with reacting to a sub/special to react to you
Stalling tower (furthering lead / preventing other team from being able to make a push)
- getting tower stalled (need to win 1v1s, NO trades, no wipes, in order to fight against the stall and to make a push)
Stalling fights (make more time to down on clock)
- getting stalled (see above)

Getting good at splatoon ranked: basic tips
- don't make a push until at least 2 ppl on the enemy team are down AND at least 3 ppl on your team are up. The more people you have up over the enemy team, the better
- Try to further your lead to get a KO, but don't be too reckless about it
- Be patient and watch your opponents—they will often rush objective without looking for you. That's when you can take advantage and splat them
- The worst thing you can do for your team is get splatted. Avoid getting splatted when you can, ie in situations where you are at a disadvantage. If you have an advantage, then be able to defend yourself
- However, the best thing to do for your team is NOT to get splats . It's to make advantageous situations for you and your teammates. Sometimes this IS in the form of splatting your opponents, but other times it can be in the form of getting your special and using it well, distracting your opponents away from objective while your teammates go for it, holding onto mid, staying alive as a super jump spot, looking for ways to help or take advantage of your teammates in a good way, pushing objective if your teammates are harassing your opponents, or just turfing. Ranked tests your decision making skills and your ability to prioritize certain things at certain points of the game. Don't panic, pay attention to your teammates, and have a plan. 
- If someone is chasing you or tracking you, try to shake them off. Bring them to your teammates but in a way where they don't know your teammates are there. That way, they can help you, instead of you getting your teammates AND you killed
- Hold onto mid before rushing into enemy base and trying to splat them as they spawn in
- If you're getting pushed back into YOUR spawn, do not rush in alone. Do not go for objective alone, especially if there is plenty of time left in the game. Stay in the same horizontal line as your teammates and push up together as a unit. Coordinate specials and stay alive, and try to win 1v1s or help your teammates out in 1v1s when you can
- Use your sub weapon to poke your opponents out of places that they want to hold onto. (Use your special weapon for this too if applicable.)
- Turf up!!! Do not just focus on splats and objective. Remember that the main gimmick of splatoon is putting ink on the ground to help your mobility. The more mobile you are, the more flexible your movements are and the more creative your choices you can be. It also limits your opponents' mobility and usually forces them to move in more predictable ways
